Khalil Gibran (/dʒɪˈbrɑːn/;[1] sometimes spelled Khalil;[a] full Arabic name Gibran Khalil Gibran, Arabic: جبران خليل جبران‎‎ / ALA-LC: Jubrān Khalīl Jubrān or Jibrān Khalīl Jibrān) (January 6, 1883 – April 10, 1931) was a Lebanese-American artist, poet, and writer of the New York Pen League.





And that that which sings and contemplates in you is still dwelling within the bounds of that first moment which scattered the stars into space.

Of time you would make a stream upon whose bank you would sit and watch its flowing. Yet the timeless in you is aware of life's timelessness, And knows that yesterday is but today's memory and tomorrow is today's dream.
